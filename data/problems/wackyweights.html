There are N weights and a balance scale sitting on a table in front of you. N-1 of the weights weigh 10 grams, but 1 of them weighs 11 grams. Ultimately, your goal is to determine which weight is the one that is 11 grams.

You can place any number of weights on the left and right sides of the scale (but you must place the same number on both the left and right side). Assume you place M weights on the left, M on the right, and leave N-2M on the table:

  1. If the scale leans left, you know the heavier weight is one of the M on the left.
  2. If the scale leans left, you know the heavier weight is one of the M on the right.
  3. If the scale stays balanced, you know the heaver weight is one of the N-2M on the table.

Given a number of weights 1 <= N <= 2000, you want to find the 11 gram weight using the scale as few times as possible. What is the most times you must use the scale?

Input

The first line will contain T - the number of test cases to follow. Each of the T remaining lines will contain a singular number N - the number of weights on the table.

Output

For each test case, print a single number on its own line: the maximum number of times you must use the scale if you employ an optimal strategy.
