Hard work pays off! Torbjorn has equipped his turret with a laser-emitting stun gun. He enters a room with mirrors on the walls and places the turret near the enemy Sombra's teleporter device. When Sombra teleports, the turret will trigger and shoot a laser beam to try to stun the Sombra. The laser beams have a maximum range R meters traveled after leaving the turret.

Torbjorn wants to know: for a given room, turret position, sombra position, and range R, how many angles exist such that a beam fired in that direction would hit Sombra? Assume that a beam travels straight except hitting a vertical/horizontal wall exactly inverts the vertical/horizontal component of the beam's velocity, respectively.

Input

Input consists of four lines:

  1. Two positive integers: the width and height of the room
  2. Two positive integers: the x and y position of Torbjorn's turret
  3. Two positiive integers: the x and y position of Sombra
  4. One non-negative float: the maximum distance a beam can travel

The turret and Sombra will always be at least one unit away from all walls, and will never share the same location.

Output

Print a single integer: the number of angles which the turret can fire a beam and hit Sombra.

Note: if a beam hits the turret or Sombra, it stops there. You cannot have a beam continue through an object then hit something else behind it. Also, both objects are considered to have no "width". For a beam to hit the object, it must be aimed exactly at the colliding object's location in the room.
